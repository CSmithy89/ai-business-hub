# Customer Discovery Workflow Instructions

<critical>Customer insights must be sourced from research, not invented</critical>
<critical>Use Jobs-to-be-Done framework for deep understanding</critical>

<workflow>

<step n="1" goal="Define Customer Segments">
<action>Identify potential customer segments</action>

<segment_criteria>
- Demographics (B2C) or Firmographics (B2B)
- Pain point severity
- Willingness and ability to pay
- Accessibility (can we reach them?)
</segment_criteria>

<output>
List 3-5 potential segments with initial prioritization
</output>
</step>

<step n="2" goal="Research Customer Data">
<action>Gather data on target segments</action>

<searches>
1. "{{target_description}} challenges problems"
2. "{{target_description}} software tools they use"
3. "{{industry}} buyer behavior research"
4. "{{target_description}} reddit forum discussion"
5. "{{product_category}} user reviews complaints"
</searches>

<sources>
- Industry research reports
- G2/Capterra/TrustPilot reviews of competitors
- Reddit/forum discussions
- LinkedIn profiles and posts
- Survey data if available
</sources>
</step>

<step n="3" goal="Jobs-to-be-Done Analysis">
<action>Apply JTBD framework to understand customer motivations</action>

<framework>
**Functional Jobs**: What task are they trying to accomplish?
**Emotional Jobs**: How do they want to feel?
**Social Jobs**: How do they want to be perceived?

**Job Statement Format**:
"When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]."
</framework>

<action>Document 5-10 jobs for primary segment</action>
<action>Rank by importance and satisfaction with current solutions</action>
</step>

<step n="4" goal="Pain Point Mapping">
<action>Document specific pain points with evidence</action>

<pain_dimensions>
- Frequency: How often does this pain occur?
- Severity: How bad is it when it happens?
- Current Cost: What does this pain cost them?
- Workarounds: What do they do about it today?
</pain_dimensions>

<evidence_sources>
- Quote from review site
- Forum post
- Survey data
- Interview transcript (if available)
</evidence_sources>
</step>

<step n="5" goal="Create Customer Personas">
<action>Develop 2-3 detailed personas based on research</action>

<persona_components>
- Name and photo description
- Demographics/Firmographics
- Role and responsibilities
- Goals and motivations
- Frustrations and pain points
- Technology proficiency
- Decision-making authority
- Typical day scenario
- Quote that captures their perspective
</persona_components>

<critical>Personas must be based on real research data, not fiction</critical>
</step>

<step n="6" goal="Buying Process Analysis">
<action>Map the customer buying journey</action>

<stages>
1. Problem Recognition: When do they realize they need a solution?
2. Information Search: Where do they look for options?
3. Evaluation: How do they compare alternatives?
4. Purchase: Who decides? What's the process?
5. Post-Purchase: How do they measure success?
</stages>

<action>Identify key touchpoints and influencers</action>
</step>

<step n="7" goal="Willingness to Pay Analysis">
<action>Research price sensitivity and value perception</action>

<research_areas>
- What do they pay for similar solutions today?
- What's the cost of the problem (ROI potential)?
- Budget authority and approval process
- Price anchors in the market
</research_areas>
</step>

<step n="8" goal="Customer Acquisition Strategy">
<action>Identify best channels to reach target customers</action>

<channels>
- Content marketing / SEO
- Paid advertising (Google, LinkedIn, Facebook)
- Community / social media
- Partnerships / integrations
- Direct sales / outbound
- Product-led growth
</channels>

<action>Estimate CAC and compare to LTV</action>
</step>

<step n="9" goal="Compile and Save">
<action>Populate template with all findings</action>
<action>Run validation checklist</action>
<action>Save customer discovery document</action>
</step>

</workflow>
